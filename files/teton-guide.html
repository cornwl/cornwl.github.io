Getting There

JAC is inside the park

Jackson Hole Airport (JAC) is the only commercial airport inside a U.S. national park — you'll see the Tetons from the runway. The drive to Heartwood Lodge in Teton Village is about 14 miles (~20–25 min).

Weather & What to Pack

Layer up

Late June / early July is prime Teton summer — long sunny days, wildflowers everywhere, and afternoon thunderstorms that roll in fast and clear out within an hour. Mornings can be genuinely cold (mid-30s°F), afternoons warm (upper 70s–low 80s°F), and evenings cool off quickly after sunset around 9 PM.

EveryoneLayers: morning fleece + afternoon t-shirt. Rain jacket (not optional). Sunscreen SPF 50+. Sunglasses. Water bottles. Comfortable walking shoes.
Activity daysClosed-toe shoes (required for horseback & rafting). Long pants for horseback ride. Quick-dry clothes for rafting. Hat for sun.
EveningsLight jacket or hoodie — the rodeo and the Aurora summit dinner get chilly. Layers for early Cowboy Coffee + Schwabacher sunrise.
KidsSun hats, warm layers for mornings, snacks for car rides. Bear spray is for adults only (available at Moose Junction or Jackson).

Teton Village & Surroundings

Heartwood Lodge sits atop Granite Ridge in Teton Village, which is a self-contained base at the foot of Jackson Hole Mountain Resort. The village has restaurants, shops, and the aerial tram (runs in summer — ride it for incredible views). The town of Jackson is about 12 miles south (~20 min). Grand Teton National Park's main corridor runs right past you.
